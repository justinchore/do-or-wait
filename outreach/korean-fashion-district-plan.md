# Korean Fashion District — Outreach Plan

**What:** Testing a new vertical — Korean-owned apparel/textile wholesalers in the LA Fashion District (Jobber Market, ~1,000 KAMA members packed into 100 blocks).

**Why it could work:** Dense, concentrated target area, and a real product fit — fast-fashion jobbers have volatile, seasonal, container-driven demand, which is exactly what flexible month-to-month, all-inclusive space is built for. Tight, referral-driven community, so one good relationship pulls in more.

**The plan (scout-first, to protect spend):**
1. Run a cheap Apollo discovery to map who's there (no email-reveal credits yet).
2. Spend one morning walking the floor as a scout — not selling, just asking owners where they keep overflow stock and whether nearby flex space would help.
3. Only if the floor confirms real off-site demand do we spend Apollo reveal credits and start outreach with drafted openers in the Outreach tab.

**Who we target:** Large jobbers + distribution/e-commerce importers (they already need off-site space). Smalls are for referrals and intel, not a campaign.

**The question the scout answers cheaply:** Do these shops need off-site space, or does everything have to stay in-building? That decides whether this scales.

**Pitch angle:** Flexibility over proximity (nearest sites: Reyes / City of Industry). Month-to-month, all-in, fast move-in vs. a scary long industrial lease.

**Status:** Business cards being ordered (Kuan). Apollo discovery run is next.
