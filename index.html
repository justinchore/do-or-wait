📇 Battle cards — quick reference

COLD CALL FLOW

Cubework Fontana · BD
flexible warehouse space
One goal on this call: book a tour. Don't try to close on the phone.
01 · 10-sec prep

Before you dial

Who am I calling & what segment (importer / 3PL / local biz / seller)? Have the unit list & sizes in front of you.
02 · Opener

Open warm — who you are, a reason, an easy out

Hi [name], this is Justin with Cubework in Fontana. We do flexible warehouse space near the ports, and I'm reaching out to local businesses to see if it's a fit. Can I ask you a couple quick questions?
Honest and respectful without putting you on the spot — the easiest one to say a hundred times. Your default while you find your footing.
Then roll straight in:
Mind if I ask what you're running for space right now?
Benefit-neutral on purpose — you don't know their pain yet. That question hands you into qualifying (step 03).
Opener menu — pick the tone that fits you
Warmest · low pressure
…I'll keep it quick, I know you're busy. We've got flexible space opening up nearby — is now a bad time?
"Is now a bad time?" is easy to answer "no, go ahead" — softer than "got a minute?"
Straightforward · default ✓
…we do flexible warehouse space near the ports, and I'm reaching out locally to see if it's a fit. Can I ask a couple quick questions?
The one shown above. Most foolproof — never feels forced.
Curiosity · warm / targeted calls
…quick question — are you running your own warehousing now, or outsourcing it? Reason I ask, we've got flexible space opening up close to you.
Gets them talking; doubles as your first qualifying question. Best when you've reason to think they use warehousing.
03 · Qualify

Ask, then shut up and listen

  • What are you running for space right now — and what's not working about it?
  • Are you bringing in containers / imports, or storing & shipping your own product?
  • What's kept you from locking something in elsewhere?
Their answer tells you which branch to take below.
04 · Branch — match the wedge to what they said
If: stuck w/ a 3PL · fussy about handling · "lost control"
CONTROL
Most flexible space here forces you onto their 3PL to get in the door. We don't. Your product, your people, your process — services optional.
If: importer · running containers · port freight
CUBESHIP
We're near LA/Long Beach and we can handle the shipping & customs on your container freight too — land it straight with us.
If: just needs space · fast · flexible terms
SPEED + FLEX
We're move-in ready now and terms are flexible — no long lease to get in. You could be operating fast.
05 · The ask

Book the tour

Easiest thing is to come see it — takes 15 minutes. Does [day] or [day] work better for you?
Offer two times, not "are you free." Make saying yes easy.

Quick objection handles

"Just send info." → Sure — but the space sells itself in person. Got 15 min this week?
"Too expensive." → What were you quoted? Terms are flexible, let's see if it fits.
"Not right now." → Totally fair. When are you re-looking? I'll check back then.
"Already have space." → Anything you're outgrowing? And know anyone who needs space?

No answer / voicemail

Leave it short: name, Cubework Fontana, flexible space available now, your number — twice. Then follow up by email same day.

After every call — log it

Name · segment · what they need · outcome · next action + date. Most deals die from no follow-up, not from "no."
REMEMBER: You're building relationships, not running up numbers. Not every call is a fit — that's fine. Listen more than you pitch, be useful, and follow up when you said you would. The space fills when people trust you.
